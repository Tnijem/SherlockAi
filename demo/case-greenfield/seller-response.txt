Dear Ms. Park:

This firm has been retained by Greenfield Industrial Partners, LLC ("Greenfield") in
connection with your letter dated November 5, 2025. We respond as follows.

Greenfield disputes the characterization of its representations as "materially false"
or constituting misrepresentation. The operative language in Section 2.3 is expressly
qualified by "to Seller's actual knowledge." Section 5.2 further defines this term
as "the current, actual (not imputed or constructive) knowledge of Marcus Webb,
Managing Member, without independent investigation or inquiry."

Mr. Webb acquired the Property through foreclosure proceedings in 2020. He was not
involved in the textile manufacturing operations (which ceased in 2003) or the UST
removal (conducted in 2004). The Phase I ESA conducted in 2020 identified potential
RECs but did not confirm contamination — that distinction is material.

With respect to the UST closure, Mr. Webb relied in good faith on the 2004 closure
report prepared by a licensed environmental professional and accepted by Georgia EPD
at that time. The fact that the closure may not meet current regulatory standards
does not retroactively render Mr. Webb's representation false — he had no knowledge
that the closure was deficient.

That said, Greenfield recognizes that the Phase II findings are significant and
wishes to resolve this matter commercially. Greenfield proposes the following:

  1. Purchase price reduced by $900,000 to $3,300,000 (reflecting the low end of
     Option C — Brownfield approach, $800,000, plus a $100,000 contingency);

  2. Seller to escrow $400,000 from proceeds to fund initial remediation activities;

  3. Seller to cooperate with Georgia Brownfield program enrollment;

  4. Environmental indemnification with a cap of $1,500,000 and 5-year survival period;

  5. Closing extended 30 days to December 14, 2025.

Greenfield is prepared to negotiate in good faith but cannot accept your client's
demand for a $2.8 million price reduction on a $4.2 million sale, which would reduce
the effective purchase price to one-third of fair market value. Greenfield's proposal
reflects a reasonable allocation of risk given the knowledge-qualified representations
and the property's as-is condition.

We respectfully request that Riverside not terminate the Agreement during negotiations
and that the Due Diligence Period be extended by 30 days by mutual agreement to allow
the parties to reach a resolution.

Please advise.
