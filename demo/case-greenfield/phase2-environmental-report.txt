1.0 EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Southern Environmental Consultants, LLC ("SEC") was retained by Riverside Development
Group, Inc. ("Client") to conduct a Phase II Environmental Site Assessment ("Phase II ESA")
of the property located at 1500 Industrial Boulevard, Macon, Georgia 31201 (the "Site").

This Phase II ESA was conducted in response to Recognized Environmental Conditions ("RECs")
identified in our Phase I ESA dated October 3, 2025, including:

  REC-1: Former textile manufacturing operations (1968-2003) with potential for chemical
         releases from dyeing and finishing processes
  REC-2: Former UST locations — closure report from 2004 indicated "minor staining"
         but no confirmation sampling was performed at that time
  REC-3: Undocumented fill material observed in the northeastern portion of the Site

CRITICAL FINDINGS:

Our investigation revealed significant soil and groundwater contamination requiring
regulatory notification and potential remediation:

  ▸ Tetrachloroethylene (PCE) in groundwater at 78 μg/L — 15.6x the EPA MCL of 5 μg/L
  ▸ Trichloroethylene (TCE) in groundwater at 23 μg/L — 4.6x the EPA MCL of 5 μg/L
  ▸ Petroleum hydrocarbons in soil at former UST location — up to 3,400 mg/kg TPH
  ▸ Lead in soil in fill area — up to 890 mg/kg (above EPA residential RSL of 400 mg/kg)
  ▸ Hexavalent chromium in soil near former dye building — 42 mg/kg

Estimated remediation cost: $1.2 million to $2.8 million depending on selected approach.

2.0 SITE DESCRIPTION AND HISTORY

2.1 Current Conditions
The Site consists of approximately 12.4 acres improved with a 185,000 square foot
industrial building constructed in 1968, with additions in 1978 and 1992. The building
is currently vacant. A paved parking area (approximately 2 acres) is located on the
western side. The eastern portion includes a loading dock area and former chemical
storage building (approximately 3,000 sf).

2.2 Historical Operations
1968-2003: Georgia Textile Mills, Inc. operated a textile manufacturing facility
including dyeing, finishing, and printing operations. Chemicals used included:
  - Tetrachloroethylene (PCE) — dry cleaning and degreasing of fabrics
  - Various dyes containing heavy metals (chromium, lead, cadmium)
  - Petroleum products (diesel fuel, hydraulic oils)
  - Acids and bases for pH adjustment of dyeing processes

2003-2018: Southeast Logistics, LLC operated a warehouse/distribution center.
Two 10,000-gallon underground storage tanks (diesel fuel) were removed in 2004.

2018-Present: Property vacant.

3.0 SCOPE OF WORK

3.1 Soil Investigation
  - 24 soil borings (SB-1 through SB-24) advanced to depths of 10-25 feet below
    ground surface (bgs) using hollow-stem auger
  - 72 soil samples collected at various depths
  - Samples analyzed for: VOCs (EPA Method 8260), SVOCs (EPA Method 8270),
    RCRA 8 Metals (EPA Method 6010/7471), TPH-GRO/DRO (EPA Methods 8015/8015),
    Hexavalent Chromium (EPA Method 7196A)

3.2 Groundwater Investigation
  - 6 monitoring wells installed (MW-1 through MW-6) at 20-30 feet bgs
  - Groundwater encountered at approximately 15 feet bgs
  - Samples analyzed for: VOCs, SVOCs, Metals, TPH

3.3 Soil Gas Investigation
  - 8 soil vapor points (SV-1 through SV-8) installed at 5 feet bgs
  - Analyzed for VOCs via EPA Method TO-15

PCE and TCE exceed Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs) at MW-1, MW-2, and MW-3.

monitored natural attenuation.

5.0 REGULATORY IMPLICATIONS

5.1 Georgia EPD Notification
    Under Georgia Hazardous Site Response Act (HSRA), O.C.G.A. § 12-8-90 et seq.,
    the property owner must notify Georgia EPD within 30 days of discovery of
    contamination exceeding reportable quantities. This notification is REQUIRED.

5.2 CERCLA Liability
    The contamination from textile manufacturing operations likely pre-dates current
    ownership. However, Greenfield Industrial Partners as current owner may have
    liability under CERCLA § 107(a)(1) as a "current owner or operator."

    Riverside Development Group should evaluate:
    - CERCLA "innocent landowner" defense (42 U.S.C. § 9601(35))
    - "Bona fide prospective purchaser" defense (42 U.S.C. § 9601(40))
    - Georgia Brownfield program enrollment for liability protection

5.3 Vapor Intrusion Pathway
    Development of residential units in the eastern portion of the Site will
    require vapor intrusion mitigation. EPA guidance (2015 OSWER Technical Guide)
    and Georgia EPD Rule 391-3-19 apply.

6.0 REMEDIATION COST ESTIMATES

Option A: Full excavation and off-site disposal (soil) + pump-and-treat (groundwater)
  Estimated cost: $2.4 - $2.8 million
  Timeline: 18-24 months active remediation + 3-5 years monitoring

Option B: In-situ chemical oxidation (soil) + monitored natural attenuation (groundwater)
  Estimated cost: $1.2 - $1.6 million
  Timeline: 6-12 months active treatment + 5-10 years monitoring

Option C: Georgia Brownfield program (risk-based approach with institutional controls)
  Estimated cost: $800,000 - $1.2 million
  Timeline: 12-18 months + long-term monitoring
  Requires: Land use restrictions (may limit residential development)

7.0 CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS

1. The Site has significant environmental contamination from historical textile
   manufacturing operations and inadequately closed USTs.

2. The contamination was NOT disclosed by Seller in the Purchase and Sale Agreement.
   Seller's environmental representations (Section 2.3) appear to be materially
   inaccurate, particularly representations (a) and (d).

3. We recommend:
   (a) Immediate notification to Georgia EPD per HSRA requirements
   (b) Do NOT close on the purchase until contamination is fully characterized
       and remediation responsibility is allocated
   (c) Re-negotiate purchase price to account for remediation costs ($1.2-$2.8M)
   (d) Require Seller to escrow remediation funds or obtain environmental insurance
   (e) Evaluate Georgia Brownfield program for liability protection
