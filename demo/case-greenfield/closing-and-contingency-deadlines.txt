BACKGROUND

This memorandum identifies and tracks all contractual deadlines arising from the
Commercial Real Estate Purchase and Sale Agreement dated October 15, 2025 (the
"Agreement") between Greenfield Commercial Properties, LLC ("Buyer") and
Harborview Development Corp. ("Seller") for the purchase of the Bayside Industrial
Park property located at 8400 Harbor Blvd., Miami, FL 33132 (the "Property").

Purchase Price: $4,750,000
Earnest Money Deposit: $237,500 (held in escrow by South Florida Title Co.)

The Agreement is currently in dispute following Seller's failure to disclose the
Phase II environmental contamination report. See attached Demand Letter dated
January 22, 2026.

PART I — CONTRACTUAL DEADLINES (from Purchase and Sale Agreement)

Section 3.2 — Inspection Period
    The Agreement provided a 45-day inspection period commencing October 15, 2025.
    The inspection period therefore expired on November 29, 2025. Buyer timely
    completed Phase I Environmental Site Assessment and structural inspection during
    this period. STATUS: EXPIRED/COMPLETED.

Section 4.1 — Financing Contingency Deadline
    Buyer's obligation to close was contingent upon obtaining financing commitment
    no later than December 15, 2025. Buyer received conditional financing commitment
    from First Southern Commercial Bank on December 12, 2025. The financing
    contingency has been satisfied.
    STATUS: SATISFIED. No further action required.

Section 5.3 — Title Objection Deadline
    Buyer had 30 days from receipt of the title commitment to raise objections.
    Title commitment was delivered November 3, 2025; objection deadline was
    December 3, 2025. No title objections were raised.
    STATUS: EXPIRED. Buyer is bound by title as-is subject to permitted exceptions.

Section 6.1 — Seller Disclosure Obligation (BREACHED)
    Seller was contractually required to provide all material environmental
    disclosures no later than 10 days after contract execution, i.e., by
    October 25, 2025. Seller failed to disclose the Phase II Environmental
    Report dated September 8, 2025 (identifying TCE contamination in excess
    of FDEP action levels). This constitutes a material breach of the Agreement.
    STATUS: BREACH DOCUMENTED. Basis for rescission/damages claim.

Section 8.1 — CLOSING DEADLINE — CRITICAL
    The Agreement requires closing to occur no later than February 28, 2026.
    Given the pending dispute over Seller's nondisclosure, Buyer served written
    notice of dispute on January 22, 2026 (within the Agreement's 30-day cure
    period). Seller has until February 21, 2026 to cure or respond.
    If dispute is not resolved, closing will not occur and Buyer will be entitled
    to return of earnest money plus damages.
    DEADLINE: February 28, 2026 — CLOSING (or formal termination by this date).
    URGENCY: CRITICAL.

Section 8.3 — Time is of the Essence
    The Agreement expressly provides that time is of the essence with respect to
    all closing and performance deadlines. Failure to close by February 28, 2026
    without mutual written extension will constitute a default.
    ACTION REQUIRED: If dispute is not resolved by February 21, 2026, file
    for emergency injunctive relief to preserve earnest money prior to closing date.

PART II — LITIGATION DEADLINES

If Buyer elects to pursue litigation for rescission and damages arising from
Seller's fraudulent concealment, the following limitations and notice deadlines apply:

    1.  Fraudulent Concealment SOL — FLORIDA STATUTE § 95.11(3)(j)
        Four-year statute of limitations for fraud. Discovery rule applies: SOL
        runs from the date Buyer discovered or should have discovered the fraud.
        Buyer first learned of the concealed environmental report on January 8, 2026,
        when Greenfield's environmental consultant independently obtained the Phase II
        report from the FDEP public records portal.
        SOL EXPIRATION: January 8, 2030.
        STATUS: OPEN — Calendar for monitoring.

    2.  Breach of Contract SOL — FLORIDA STATUTE § 95.11(2)(b)
        Five-year statute of limitations for written contract claims.
        Agreement executed October 15, 2025.
        SOL EXPIRATION: October 15, 2030.
        STATUS: OPEN — Calendar for monitoring.

    3.  Florida Deceptive and Unfair Trade Practices Act (FDUTPA)
        FDUTPA claims under § 501.204 carry a four-year SOL.
        Discovery rule applies; SOL runs from January 8, 2026.
        SOL EXPIRATION: January 8, 2030.
        Note: FDUTPA provides for attorney's fees to prevailing party — evaluate
        whether to include in complaint.
        STATUS: OPEN — Evaluate for inclusion.

    4.  Demand Response Deadline — CONTRACT NOTICE
        Buyer's Demand Letter of January 22, 2026 required Seller to respond and
        cure within 30 days, per Agreement § 14.2. Seller's response is due
        no later than February 21, 2026.
        If no response by February 21, 2026, Buyer may terminate Agreement and
        demand return of earnest money within 5 business days.
        DEADLINE: February 21, 2026 — SELLER CURE/RESPONSE DUE. HIGH PRIORITY.

    5.  Earnest Money Escrow Release Deadline — CONTRACT/NOTICE
        South Florida Title Co.'s escrow agreement requires both parties to provide
        joint instructions for release, or a court order, within 60 days of any
        dispute notice. Dispute notice was served January 22, 2026. If no joint
        instructions are received, escrow agent will interplead the funds into court
        no later than March 23, 2026.
        DEADLINE: March 23, 2026 — ESCROW INTERPLEADER DEADLINE.
        ACTION: If dispute unresolved, file motion to confirm entitlement to
        earnest money prior to March 23, 2026.

PART III — REGULATORY DEADLINES

    6.  FDEP Voluntary Cleanup Tax Credit Application
        If Buyer takes title and undertakes environmental remediation, Florida's
        Voluntary Cleanup Tax Credit program (§ 376.30781) requires application
        within 12 months of beginning remediation activities. This deadline is
        triggered only if Buyer proceeds with acquisition.
        STATUS: CONTINGENT. Monitor.

    7.  EPA CERCLA Notice — 60-Day Notice Requirement
        If Buyer pursues cost recovery against Seller under CERCLA § 107 for
        remediation costs, Buyer must provide 60-day advance notice to EPA and
        state environmental agency before filing suit.
        DEADLINE: Must serve 60-day notice at least 60 days before any CERCLA
        complaint is filed. Triggered upon decision to file federal environmental claim.
