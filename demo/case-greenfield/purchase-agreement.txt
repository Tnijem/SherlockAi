RECITALS

WHEREAS, Seller is the owner of that certain real property located at 1500 Industrial
Boulevard, Macon, Georgia 31201, consisting of approximately 12.4 acres of land improved
with a 185,000 square foot industrial facility (the "Property"); and

WHEREAS, Buyer desires to purchase the Property for redevelopment as a mixed-use
commercial and residential complex known as "Riverside Commons"; and

WHEREAS, the parties desire to set forth the terms and conditions of such sale;

NOW, THEREFORE, for good and valuable consideration, the parties agree as follows:

ARTICLE 1 — PURCHASE PRICE AND PAYMENT

1.1 Purchase Price: $4,200,000 (Four Million Two Hundred Thousand Dollars)

1.2 Earnest Money: $210,000 deposited with First National Title Company within 5 business
    days of execution. Non-refundable after expiration of the Due Diligence Period except
    in the event of Seller's default.

1.3 Balance: $3,990,000 due at Closing by wire transfer of immediately available funds.

ARTICLE 2 — DUE DILIGENCE

2.1 Due Diligence Period: Buyer shall have sixty (60) days from the Effective Date
    (the "Due Diligence Period") to conduct inspections, environmental assessments,
    surveys, and other investigations of the Property.

2.2 Environmental Assessment: Buyer shall conduct, at Buyer's expense:
    (a) Phase I Environmental Site Assessment in accordance with ASTM E1527-21
    (b) If warranted by Phase I findings, Phase II Environmental Site Assessment
    (c) Any additional testing Buyer deems necessary

2.3 Seller's Representations Regarding Environmental Condition:
    Seller represents and warrants to Buyer that, to Seller's actual knowledge:
    (a) No Hazardous Substances have been released, stored, or disposed of on the Property
        in violation of applicable Environmental Laws;
    (b) The Property is not listed on the National Priorities List (NPL), the CERCLIS
        database, or any state equivalent;
    (c) Seller has not received any notice from any governmental authority regarding
        environmental violations or required remediation;
    (d) All underground storage tanks on the Property have been properly removed or
        closed in accordance with applicable regulations;
    (e) Seller has provided Buyer with all environmental reports in Seller's possession.

2.4 Termination Right: If Buyer is not satisfied with the results of due diligence,
    Buyer may terminate this Agreement by written notice to Seller before expiration
    of the Due Diligence Period, in which case the Earnest Money shall be returned
    to Buyer in full.

ARTICLE 3 — SELLER'S DISCLOSURES

3.1 Seller has disclosed the following regarding the Property's history:
    (a) The Property was operated as a textile manufacturing facility from 1968 to 2003
        by Georgia Textile Mills, Inc.
    (b) From 2003 to 2018, the Property was used as a warehouse and distribution center
        by Southeast Logistics, LLC.
    (c) The Property has been vacant since 2018.
    (d) Seller acquired the Property via foreclosure in 2020.
    (e) Two underground storage tanks (USTs) were removed in 2004 by Southeast Logistics.
        Seller has provided the closure report from Georgia EPD.

3.2 Seller has provided Buyer with the following documents:
    (a) Phase I ESA dated March 2020 (prepared for Seller's acquisition)
    (b) UST closure report dated September 2004
    (c) Georgia EPD correspondence regarding UST removal (2004)
    (d) Property survey dated January 2020
    (e) Title commitment from First National Title Company

ARTICLE 4 — CLOSING

4.1 Closing Date: Within thirty (30) days after expiration of the Due Diligence Period,
    or November 14, 2025.

4.2 Closing Documents: [Standard provisions omitted for brevity]

ARTICLE 5 — REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

5.1 Seller represents and warrants:
    (a) Seller has full authority to execute this Agreement and convey the Property.
    (b) To Seller's actual knowledge, the Property is in compliance with all applicable
        laws, ordinances, and regulations.
    (c) There are no pending or threatened lawsuits, condemnation proceedings, or
        governmental actions affecting the Property.
    (d) The representations in Section 2.3 are true and correct as of the date hereof
        and shall be true and correct at Closing.

5.2 "Seller's actual knowledge" means the current, actual (not imputed or constructive)
    knowledge of Marcus Webb, Managing Member of Greenfield Industrial Partners, LLC,
    without independent investigation or inquiry.

ARTICLE 6 — REMEDIES

6.1 If Seller defaults, Buyer may: (a) seek specific performance, or (b) terminate
    this Agreement and recover the Earnest Money plus actual damages.

6.2 If Buyer defaults after the Due Diligence Period, Seller's sole remedy is retention
    of the Earnest Money as liquidated damages.

6.3 Survival: Seller's representations and warranties shall survive Closing for a
    period of two (2) years.

ARTICLE 7 — INDEMNIFICATION

7.1 Seller shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Buyer from and against any and
    all claims, losses, damages, liabilities, and expenses arising from:
    (a) Any breach of Seller's representations or warranties;
    (b) Any environmental contamination existing on the Property prior to Closing;
    (c) Any third-party claims relating to Seller's ownership or operation of the Property.

7.2 The indemnification obligations in Section 7.1 shall survive Closing for the same
    period as the underlying representations and warranties.

ARTICLE 8 — MISCELLANEOUS

8.1 Governing Law: State of Georgia
8.2 Dispute Resolution: Binding arbitration per the AAA Commercial Rules
8.3 Entire Agreement: This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties.
8.4 Amendment: No amendment except in writing signed by both parties.
