This firm represents Riverside Development Group, Inc. ("Riverside") in connection
with the above-referenced transaction. I write to inform you that the Phase II
Environmental Site Assessment of the Property has revealed severe contamination
that was not disclosed and is directly contrary to the environmental representations
made by Greenfield Industrial Partners, LLC ("Greenfield") in the Purchase Agreement.

ENVIRONMENTAL FINDINGS

Southern Environmental Consultants, LLC completed a Phase II ESA on October 28, 2025,
which revealed the following contamination at the Property:

  • Tetrachloroethylene (PCE) in groundwater at 15.6 times the EPA drinking water
    standard (78 μg/L vs. MCL of 5 μg/L)
  • Trichloroethylene (TCE) in groundwater at 4.6 times the EPA standard
  • Petroleum hydrocarbons in soil at 10 times the Georgia residential screening level
    (3,400 mg/kg at the former UST locations)
  • Lead contamination at 2.2 times the EPA residential screening level (890 mg/kg)
  • Hexavalent chromium at 145 times the residential screening level (42 mg/kg)
  • Vapor intrusion risk requiring mitigation for any enclosed structures

Estimated remediation costs range from $1.2 million to $2.8 million.

BREACH OF REPRESENTATIONS

Section 2.3 of the Purchase Agreement contains specific environmental representations.
Our investigation indicates that at least two of these representations are materially false:

  Section 2.3(a): Seller represented that "No Hazardous Substances have been released,
  stored, or disposed of on the Property in violation of applicable Environmental Laws."

  FACT: The Property has extensive contamination from chlorinated solvents, petroleum
  hydrocarbons, lead, and hexavalent chromium — all Hazardous Substances under CERCLA
  and Georgia HSRA — from decades of textile manufacturing and inadequately closed USTs.

  Section 2.3(d): Seller represented that "All underground storage tanks on the Property
  have been properly removed or closed in accordance with applicable regulations."

  FACT: The 2004 UST closure report provided by Seller documented "minor soil staining"
  but no confirmation sampling was performed. Our Phase II ESA confirmed petroleum
  contamination at 10x the applicable screening level at the former UST locations.
  The closure was NOT "in accordance with applicable regulations" — Georgia EPD UST
  regulations (Rules 391-3-15-.10) require confirmation sampling to demonstrate that
  contamination does not exceed applicable standards.

Additionally, the Phase I ESA prepared for Greenfield's 2020 acquisition of the Property
(which Greenfield provided to Riverside) identified these same RECs. It is difficult to
understand how Greenfield could represent "no knowledge" of potential contamination when
its own Phase I ESA flagged the risks.

DEMAND

Riverside hereby demands that Greenfield, within ten (10) business days of this letter:

  1. ACKNOWLEDGE the material misrepresentation in the environmental warranties;

  2. AGREE to reduce the purchase price by $2,800,000 (reflecting the high-end
     remediation cost estimate) to a revised price of $1,400,000; OR alternatively,

  3. AGREE to escrow $2,800,000 from the purchase proceeds to fund remediation,
     with terms to be negotiated; OR alternatively,

  4. AGREE to conduct remediation at Seller's expense prior to Closing, with Closing
     date extended accordingly;

  5. In any scenario, AGREE to full environmental indemnification of Riverside with
     no cap and a 10-year survival period;

  6. NOTIFY Georgia EPD of the contamination as required by HSRA within 30 days
     of the Phase II ESA (by November 27, 2025).
