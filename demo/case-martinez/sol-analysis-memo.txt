I. CRITICAL DEADLINE SUMMARY

This memo analyzes all applicable statutes of limitations and notice deadlines
arising from Maria Elena Martinez's medical malpractice claim against Coastal
Healthcare Systems and Dr. Raymond Thornton. Given the incident date of
September 14, 2023, several deadlines are approaching imminently or have
already been addressed through the filing of the Complaint on March 14, 2025.

URGENT: The following deadlines require immediate calendar verification:

    1.  Medical Malpractice SOL — CRITICAL DEADLINE
        Florida Statute § 95.11(4)(b) imposes a two-year statute of limitations
        for medical malpractice claims, running from the date the incident was
        discovered or should have been discovered through the exercise of due
        diligence. Ms. Martinez underwent the procedure on September 14, 2023.
        The discovery date is established as October 2, 2023 (date of second
        surgical consultation confirming the perforation). The SOL therefore
        EXPIRED or will EXPIRE on October 2, 2025.
        STATUS: Complaint filed March 14, 2025 — SOL PRESERVED. No action needed.

    2.  Pre-Suit Notice Requirement — SATISFIED
        Florida Statute § 766.106 requires a claimant to serve a Notice of Intent
        to Initiate Litigation at least 90 days prior to filing suit. Our Notice
        of Intent was served on all defendants on December 6, 2024. The 90-day
        pre-suit investigation period expired on March 6, 2025. Complaint timely
        filed March 14, 2026.
        STATUS: SATISFIED. No further action required on this deadline.

    3.  Wrongful Death Contingency — WATCH
        In the event Ms. Martinez's condition deteriorates and a wrongful death
        claim becomes available, Florida Statute § 95.11(4)(d) provides a two-year
        statute of limitations running from the date of death. Counsel should monitor
        client's medical status. If prognosis changes, this deadline must be
        immediately calendared.
        STATUS: OPEN — Calendar immediately upon any change in medical status.

    4.  Claim Against Hospital Credentialing Department — EVALUATE
        To the extent we pursue a negligent credentialing theory against Coastal
        Healthcare for granting privileges to Dr. Thornton despite his prior
        disciplinary history, the limitations period under § 95.11(4)(b) runs from
        the date of discovery. Ms. Martinez had no knowledge of Dr. Thornton's
        prior disciplinary record until our investigator obtained the records on
        January 15, 2026. Accordingly, the SOL on the negligent credentialing
        claim does not expire until January 15, 2028.
        STATUS: OPEN — Must be added to Complaint via amendment by April 15, 2026,
        or filed as a separate action. See FRCP 15(a).

    5.  Amendment to Add New Defendant — FILING DEADLINE
        If we intend to add Coastal Healthcare's parent corporation, MedStar Group
        LLC, as an additional defendant based on the alter ego theory identified in
        the forensic accountant's preliminary report, we must file a Motion for
        Leave to Amend Complaint no later than April 15, 2026, consistent with
        the Court's scheduling order. After that date, amendment requires a showing
        of good cause under FRCP 16(b)(4).
        DEADLINE: April 15, 2026 — HIGH PRIORITY.

II. FLORIDA § 766.106 PRE-SUIT REQUIREMENTS — ANALYSIS

Florida law imposes a mandatory pre-suit process for medical malpractice claims
that runs concurrently with the SOL analysis above. The following notice requirements
governed the pre-suit period:

    a.  90-Day Investigation Period: Defendants had 90 days from receipt of Notice
        of Intent to conduct investigation and respond. Period ran December 6, 2024
        through March 6, 2025. All defendants rejected the claim within the period.

    b.  Mandatory Presuit Mediation: Florida § 766.108 requires mandatory presuit
        mediation if either party requests it within the 90-day investigation period.
        Neither party requested presuit mediation. This obligation has lapsed.

    c.  Medical Expert Affidavit: Complaint was required to be accompanied by a
        verified written medical expert opinion. Dr. Patricia Williams's affidavit
        was attached to the Complaint as Exhibit A. SATISFIED.

III. COLLATERAL DEADLINES — INSURANCE AND NOTICE

    6.  Med-Pay Insurance Lien Notice — UPCOMING
        Coastal Healthcare's insurer, MedShield National, served a notice of lien
        on February 28, 2026 asserting a $147,200 subrogation lien against any
        recovery. Under Florida § 768.76, we must notify MedShield of any settlement
        discussions no later than 30 days before executing any settlement agreement.
        This obligation becomes active upon entering settlement negotiations.
        STATUS: ACTIVE — Must notify insurer 30 days before any settlement. Calendar
        this as a triggered deadline upon commencement of any settlement discussion.

    7.  Medicare/Medicaid Notice — UPCOMING
        Ms. Martinez is enrolled in Medicaid. We must provide notice to the Florida
        Agency for Health Care Administration (AHCA) of any lawsuit affecting a
        Medicaid recipient. That notice should have been provided within 30 days of
        filing the Complaint (i.e., by April 14, 2025). Confirm with paralegal team
        whether AHCA notice was sent. If not, send immediately — this is an
        OVERDUE obligation that could affect our ability to distribute any recovery.
        DEADLINE: OVERDUE — Confirm compliance immediately.
