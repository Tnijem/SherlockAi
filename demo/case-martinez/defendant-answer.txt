Defendants Coastal Healthcare Systems, Inc. and Dr. James R. Thornton, by and through their undersigned attorneys, Waverly, Drake & Kimball LLP, hereby answer the Complaint and assert the following affirmative defenses:

ANSWER TO ALLEGATIONS

1-3. Admitted as to identities of the parties. Denied that Dr. Thornton was negligent.

4-5. Admitted as to jurisdiction and venue.

6. Admitted that Mrs. Martinez was admitted for elective cholecystectomy on January 15, 2026.

7. Admitted that pre-operative ultrasound was performed. The imaging results speak for themselves.

8. DENIED. Dr. Thornton performed the cholecystectomy in accordance with the standard of care. Bile duct injury is a known complication of laparoscopic cholecystectomy that can occur even in the absence of negligence.

9. DENIED. The intraoperative findings did not suggest bile duct injury. The post-operative course was initially unremarkable.

10. Admitted that Mrs. Martinez returned to the emergency department on January 19, 2026. The medical records speak for themselves as to clinical findings.

11-13. The medical records speak for themselves.

14. DENIED. Dr. Thornton performed appropriate dissection of the hepatocystic triangle consistent with the standard of care. The critical view of safety was achieved, although the operative note does not contain the specific phrase "critical view of safety."

15. DENIED. An intraoperative cholangiogram was not indicated as the anatomy was clear during the procedure.

16. DENIED as characterized. The operative note is consistent with standard documentation practices.

17-20. DENIED as to all allegations of negligence and breach of the standard of care.

21-24. DENIED. Dr. Thornton is an independent contractor, not an employee of Coastal Healthcare Systems.

25-27. DENIED. Coastal Healthcare Systems maintains a robust quality assurance program that meets or exceeds industry standards. Prior peer review proceedings are privileged and confidential under Florida Statute § 766.101.

28. Defendants are without knowledge or information sufficient to form a belief as to the amount of damages claimed.

AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSES

FIRST AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE: The Plaintiff's injuries, if any, were the result of a known complication of laparoscopic cholecystectomy and did not result from any negligence on the part of the Defendants.

SECOND AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE: The bile duct injury rate for laparoscopic cholecystectomy is 0.3-0.7% even in the hands of experienced surgeons. The occurrence of this complication does not, in itself, establish negligence.

THIRD AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE: Plaintiff's pre-existing conditions, including obesity (BMI 31.2), diabetes, and chronic inflammation from recurrent biliary colic, contributed to anatomic distortion that increased the surgical complexity and risk of complication.

FOURTH AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE: Plaintiff failed to mitigate damages by delaying her return to the emergency department for four days despite post-operative instructions to seek immediate care for fever, jaundice, or worsening abdominal pain.

FIFTH AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE: Dr. Thornton is an independent contractor with his own medical practice and is not an employee of Coastal Healthcare Systems. Coastal is therefore not vicariously liable for Dr. Thornton's actions.

SIXTH AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE: Coastal Healthcare Systems' peer review records are privileged and immune from discovery under Florida Statute § 766.101 (peer review privilege).

SEVENTH AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE: Defendants reserve the right to amend this Answer and assert additional affirmative defenses as discovery progresses.
